01Why Sonder Exists

People hire
people.

A note from our founder, Savana Maxon

After years inside growing organizations, I kept seeing the same pattern: companies treated people as resources to optimize instead of humans to understand. The result was predictable — burnout, turnover, and systems that looked good on paper but failed in practice.

Then I found a word for what was missing. Sonder — the realization that every person is living a life as vivid and complex as your own. It changed how I walked into every room. The person across the table was no longer a function to fill. They were a full human being with a story I'd never fully know.

Sonder Operations is what happens when you build a company around that truth — designing the operational, people, and leadership systems that let organizations grow without asking their people to compensate for what was never built for them.

Savana Maxon Founder & Principal

son·der  (n.)  the profound realization that every passerby is living a life as vivid, complex, and chaotic as your own — each the main character of their own story, while a mere extra in yours.

02The 10 Pillars

Ten domains of
organizational health.

Every engagement begins with a diagnostic. These ten pillars are the lens — each one scored, the gaps prioritized, and the work built around what actually needs to change. Together they form the complete picture of where an organization is today and where it needs to go.

01HR Technology & AI IntegrationEliminate manual work without creating chaos.Is your tech stack ready for AI — and are your people?
02Talent Acquisition & Workforce PlanningPut the right people in the right roles before you feel the gap.Do you have the right people, in the right roles, at the right time?
03Process Documentation & Workflow DesignTurn tribal knowledge into systems that survive any departure.Are your processes documented, repeatable, and improvable?
04Compensation & Total RewardsRetain high performers without overpaying.Does your pay structure attract and retain the talent you need?
05Performance ManagementGive every person a clear definition of success — and a way to measure it.Do your people know what success looks like — and do you measure it?
06Learning & DevelopmentBuild capability as fast as the market demands.Is your organization building capability as fast as the market demands?
07Organizational Design & StructureCreate clarity around ownership, accountability, and decision-making.Is your org chart aligned to strategy — or fighting it?
08Change Management & AdoptionMake change that actually sticks after we leave.Can your organization actually change — and will people follow?
09AI Governance & Ethics PolicyAdopt AI with guardrails, not guesswork.Do you have guardrails for how AI is used in your organization?
10Measurement, Reporting & Continuous ImprovementMeasure what matters — and improve from what you learn.Are you measuring what matters — and improving from what you learn?
03Core Values

Not aspirations.
Operating standards.

Every engagement, every hire, and every decision is held to these. They are how we earn the right to be trusted with the work that actually matters.

i

Empathy

We reckon with the fact that every person we work with is living a life as full as our own. We don't design around the average. We design around the actual.

ii

Authenticity

We don't perform or polish over discomfort. We bring our full selves to the work — because trust is the foundation of every outcome we produce.

iii

Adaptability

No two organizations are the same. We listen first, then design. The system should fit the organization, not the other way around.

iv

Growth Mindset

We are relentlessly curious. We can't ask our clients to grow in ways we are not willing to grow ourselves. Learning is a permanent condition.

v

Resilience

We don't avoid difficulty. We walk toward it with our clients — because the hardest moments in a business are usually the most important ones to get right.

vi

Integrity

We say what we mean and do what we say. We don't tell clients what they want to hear when what they need to hear is different.
